Donor Rights & Consent PolicyThe ethical legitimacy of organoid intelligence rests on the transparency of the relationship between the biological donor and the computational output.1. Tiered Consent FrameworkStandard biobanking consent is insufficient for systems that may acquire adaptive agency. We implement a Tiered Consent Model:Level 1: Research & Diagnostics: Use of cells for basic disease modeling and drug screening.Level 2: Computational Integration: Reprogramming cells into neural tissues for use in biocomputing experiments.Level 3: Device Commercialization: Use of derivatives in commercial hardware or cloud-accessible research platforms.2. Genetic Privacy and OwnershipWhile tissues are highly transformed during the maturation process, they retain donor-derived genetic information.Secondary Use Disclosure: Donors must be informed if their tissues are repurposed for tasks such as robotic control or environmental simulation.Withdrawal Limits: Donors are entitled to transparency regarding the point at which anonymized derivatives become integral to a system and can no longer be withdrawn.3. Escalation TriggersEnhanced ethical review is required whenever the biological complexity materially changes:Integration into advanced sensory-motor closed loops.Transplantation into animal hosts for in vivo maturation.Manifestation of unexpected adaptive agency or sentience-like properties.
